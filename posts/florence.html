Sweet Escape to Florence

March 27, 2023

← Back to all articles

Florence or Italian Firenze, is the capital of Tuscany region in central Italy. It’s actually the main and biggest city here, next to Siena, Pisa, Lucca… It’s widely known for its Renaissance Art & Culture, multiple museums and historical center distinguished by the famous Duomo. I love this city and I think there’s much more to experience here.

Florence is like a beautiful painting to me: daring and deep, yet colourful and joyful as a flower market. With its kitsch vibes and artistic soul, it’s a time-travel to past eras… In the meantime, it’s quite trendy and inspiring for modern art and design. It’s a timeless city.

Grosso modo, it’s a great destination for culture and art lovers, epicureans, fashionistas and vintage lovers… Enjoy ❤️

Ciao Firenze!

When I land in Florence, it’s just like I landed in an open-sky museum, in the 50’s ! Strolling along the pastel buildings around the Arno river and its bridges is like walking in a movie scene. Ponte Vecchio or Old Bridge is the oldest bridge since medieval age that survived World War II.

The historical center is registered in the Unesco world heritage. It’s quite impressive how every street and monument has its own story and significance. I just can’t count the number of sculptures everywhere. The open-air art-works, the churches and castles, the Italian gardens and courts.. The monuments and museums.. all describing the Renaissance Era and telling its stories… I had to make a choice which ones to visit! 😉

Art-Break

I visited La Galleria Degli Uffizi, and I enjoyed a lot Botticelli collection as well as other Italian painters like Da Vinci. At the end of your visit, you can enjoy the view over the Duomo in the museum rooftop bar.

I also visited La Galleria dell’Accademia and I was way less impressed. It’s known for Michelangelo art works, like the original sculpture of David. It’s actually the main ‘tourist-attraction’ here. Instead of queuing, you can see the copy of David’s sculpture in Piazza della Signoria, next to Fontana del Nettuno (Fontain of Neptune).

Museum Tip Book your tickets online and choose your slot, instead of wasting time queuing!

Un caffè per favore ! ☕️

It’s time for a coffee break. 😁 Let’s go grab some espresso at the bar of the cafeteria or pasticceria in the corner. I love trying italian mini-pastries, mainly with pistacchio and fragola (strawberry). The flavors are so fresh!

It’s actually a local habit to gather in the morning around the bar and have a quick standing breakfast with friends or colleagues, chatting and laughing out loud! On the other side of the cafeteria, there are those who prefer to sit quietly by their own, reading the morning newspaper. Yes, Florentines still read news in the morning instead of scrolling the phone. 😉

Cafeteria Tip If you want to stand around the bar, you need to pay and present the receipt to the barista, to be served. On the other hand, if you have more time and want to sit in your table, you can order via a waiter and take a sit, payment is done afterwards. 🤓

It seems like it’s the Italian motto.. anyways, when in Florence, and in Italy in general, eating is always fun, full of flavors and colors!

There’s a large choice of places where to eat, it depends only upon you. 😁 For a quick meal, go for street-food places like Pizzeria, Paninoteca (for paninis) or Mercato Centrale.. For an Aperitivo, Enoteca (wine and sandwiches bar) sounds good. To try traditional local recipes, go for Osteria (small traditional restaurant), or Trattoria (traditional family restaurant bigger than Osteria).. and if you look for a fancy restaurant, choose a nice Ristorante.

When in a Ristorante, don’t be surprised by he length of the menu.. As a formal Italian meal starts with Antipasto (appetizer or starter), then Primo Piatto (pasta or risotto), then Secondo Piatto (meat or fish), after that Contorno (Veggies or salad), aaaaaand finally to end on a high note : il Dolci (dessert).

Here’s the local food I enjoyed : traditional Tuscan bread soup Ribollita, Taligatelle Al Tartufo (Truffles pasta), Melanzana alla Parmigiana (Eggplant Parmesan), and of course Tiramisu, the best dessert of all times! 😊

If you love to cook and would like to extend the pleasure, you can book a Cooking Lesson in Mercato Centrale with a chef to make your favorite recipe. 👩‍🍳 😉

Italian Appeal

Florence is a great destination for fashionistas as well. Whether in the Mercato, in the small Leather shops or in the fancy brands stores, there are lots of gems for every taste and every budget!

There are also lots of interesting second-hand shops and Vintage clothing stores, Dolce Vita style! 😉

The other side of the River

If you’ve had enough of the busy center, take the bus C1 or cross the Arno via Ponte Vecchio to reach the Oltrarno, a more authentic area, less crowded and really nice to discover. We also climbed to the top to enjoy a panoramic view of the terracotta roofs of the city. 🧡

Some food spots I would share with a friend:

  • Canto De’ Nelli
  • Osteria Lungarno
  • Trattoria Nerone
  • Gamberini Pasticceria
