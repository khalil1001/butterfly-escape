Flirting with Paris

October 25, 2021

← Back to all articles

The city of love, beauty, fashion, lights and umbrellas.. the frenchies carrying home their “baguette”, cheese and wine and wearing their french hat “béret”.. these are the clichés that we always hear about Paris.

Well, when I came to Paris for the first time, I felt lost in the big city, with its complex metro map, constant traffic and people always in hurry, it was not love at first sight! I actually felt small in front of huge buildings and monuments of Renaissance, and embarrassed by the lunatic wheather.. Not surprising for a meditrranean 😉

Then I visited the lovely village of Montmartre, the bohemian corner of artists on hillside with totally different style and away from the noisy city, and I was completely enchanted!

I started looking around every corner and detail and I couldn’t be less impressed by the good energy everywhere, the museums and bookstores in every district, the dazzling city lights and vintage lampposts, the parisians chilling and enjoying life by evening (Apero time), the cosmopolitan modern society… Anyways, when I took the time to explore it and look deep into its details, Paris is actually such a vibe for epicurians “bons vivants”, and especially for ambitious and curious souls, dreamers, art and architecture lovers.

After several years of parisian life, I feel like there are still lots of things to see and do.. I’m an eternal tourist in Paris.

I love strolling around its streets, wandering along its beautiful bridges and romancing its cobblestones. Whenever it starts to rain (or snow), I go grab some coffee in the next coffeeshop or jump in the nearest subway station “bouche de métro” to reach some art gallery or photography exposition..

If you are lucky enough to be in Paris on a sunny day, please don’t miss going along the Seine river bridges.

Here’s my way of enjoying the good weather days in Paris and flirting with its bridges, there are more than 20 bridges around the Seine river between 4th and 15th district. Let’s start around Île Saint-Louis where you can find a bunch of nice ones: Pont Louis-Philippe, Pont d’Arcole, Pont Saint-Michel, Pont-Neuf and Pont des Arts (Bridge of lovers). After that, near the Louvre museum, there’s Pont du Carroussel just before Quai François Mittérand. Go for a good walk until reaching the famous bridge Pont Alexandre 3. Then, Pont des Invalides, Pont de l’Alma, Pont d’Iéna and Pont Bir-Hakeim in front of the Iron Lady.😉

In between, you can sit for a pique-nique, chill in some terrasse or treat yourself with sweet french pastry. Enjoy 😊
