Souls of Tunisia

August 2, 2023

← Back to all articles

I love travelling, it’s not a secret anymore! When I visit a new place, I always find myself amazed by new experiences and landscapes, humans with different backgrounds and different lifestyles… every little detail is part of the journey..

I spent my latest summer holidays in my homeland Tunisia, and I tried to explore more of its gems as I had special visitors. Those moments were like a gentle reminder to me on how genuinely beautiful and unique this country is. You know, that kind of beauty locals take for granted as they are used to see it everyday…

In this article, I chose a collection of beautiful moments you would experience if you visit Tunisia in summertime. It all depends on your affinities. Enjoy 😉

Dolce Far-Niente the Tunisian way 🌶️

If you look for a chill and budget-friendly holidays, Tunisia would be a great idea. With its sunny weather, its Mediterranean coast and food… it’s the place to be to relax and enjoy « slow life ». As most of Mediterranean countries, you can be in a state of «Dolce Farniente », the « art of doing nothing ».

Primo, choose wisely your accommodation deal, maybe in a fancy « all-in resort » or in a charming « Guest House ».. Secundo, plan your visit to fully enjoy the trip. Please be a « traveller » rather than an « ordinary tourist ». Be curious and greedy for its beauty and rich culture.

Small country, big civilization🇹🇳

If we look back to more than 3000 years, Tunisia has been part of great civilizations. To me, it’s like a gorgeous warrior lady, who never gave up on life! It has been Phoenician, Carthaginian, Roman, Berber, Ottoman, Arab, and finally occupied by the French… Genetically speaking, it’s a beautiful mess, so if you try to recognize Tunisian people only by physical identification, good luck with that! 😁

Blue paradise 💙

Overall, It’s a small country where you can reach different areas within 2 hours ride on average. The coastline on eastern Tunisia is where you can find wide range of golden sandy beaches and turquoise waters (Bizerte, Tunis, Cap Bon & Hammamet, Sousse, Monastir, Mahdia and Djerba). The North-Western side (like Tabarka) is about stunning green landscapes between mountains, wild beaches and high roads.

If you are a Sea-Lover..

You will be in your element… 🐠

  • Book a boat day from Marina of Hammamet or Sousse
  • Book a boat day from Djerba to discover Pink Flamingo Island (Rass Rmal)
  • Take a ride to enjoy Cap Bon beaches like Kelibia, Haouaria
  • Spend the day chilling in the Haouaria caves, a stunning spot and almost the last point before Italian coast
  • Enjoy camping life in Galite islands, the uninhabited rocky volcanic islands in Bizerte (camping events are managed by local agencies only)
  • Book a beach day in one of Ghar El Melh beach clubs and enjoy its crystal clear waters

If you are a food-lover..

Most of the food is local and seasonal. And every region is known for its own products. At the entrance of such cities, you will have a clue on its special thing. For example, in Nabeul, you will see Oranges and Pottery. Damn, they should add pepper too! 😁Actually, Harissa, the Tunisian pepper paste is an integral part of local culinary food traditions. And it’s inscribed on the UNESCO Cultural Heritage of Humanity.

There are lots of colorful food to taste:

  • Tunisian Dishes like : Shakshouka ; Ojja ; Mloukheya ; Kammouneya ; Fish Couscous ; Fish Pasta ; Djerbian Rice ; Tuna Brick…
  • Sea food 🦐 mainly in the fishing villages of the coastline
  • Street Food based on Harissa and Tuna like : Fricassé ; Mlewi ; Chapati ; Lablabi…
  • Sweet street food like : Balbalouni

If you are a Coffee/Tea-lover..

Taking a coffee or tea break is part of daily life. Tunisians love gatherings whether it’s family, friends or neighbors… It’s the moment to meet and chat about anything and everything, over a generous nice table made up of caffeine/theine and sweet colorful delights!

  • Direct, coffee with milk for your breaky, and unlike the « French café-crème » that has foam. You can choose « Direct with or without foam » when ordering 😉
  • Capucin, coffee with (less) milk, something between café-noisette and cappuccino
  • Turkish coffee and Mint Tea are especially served when receiving at home on the afternoon
  • Mint Tea is traditionally served with Pine Nuts (so delicious!)
  • If it’s too hot for those, try a fresh Lemon Juice « Citronnade » (it’s even more delicious with white Almonds!)

If you are a sunset-lover..

You will enjoy the most spectacular sunsets ever! Chase them wherever you go:

  • on the beach (especially in Djerba)
  • on top of Sidi Bou Said hill over the port of Tunis
  • from a rooftop in Marsa/Gammarth
  • from a rooftop in Medina of Tunis
  • while enjoying a walk over the shores of the Lake of Tunis

If you are an architecture lover..

There are so many spots mainly in the capital Tunis. My favorite ones are the following 3 sites that are all inscribed in the UNESCO’s world heritage.

  • The archaeological site of Carthage, founded by the Phoenicians and located on a hill dominating the Gulf of Tunis. You can visit the ancient Punic port, the Acropolium or the Saint-Louis Cathedral, the roman Catholic Church on the top of Byrsa hill… the Antonine baths, one of the three largest Roman thermae built in the Roman Empire, with a stunning sea view.
  • The historical old town or Medina of Tunis, the labyrinth of narrow cobblestone noisy streets full of colors and flavors, there are monuments like Zitouna Mosque, traditional shops, cafes, rooftops, traditional houses and restaurants..
  • Sidi Bou Said, the picturesque village perched on a cliff, overlooking the port of Tunis, get lost in its blue and white streets and admire its Arab-Andalusian facades. You can visit Dar Annabi to dive in traditional house with most of its details, and Ennejma Ezzahra palace, or the Palace of Baron d’Erlanger for a more fancy luxury Tunisian residence.
