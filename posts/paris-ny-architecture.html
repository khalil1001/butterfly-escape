From Paris to New York, Look Up!

March 29, 2024

← Back to all articles

Lost in big cities

While strolling the streets of Paris, I have always been fascinated by parisian architecture.

Buildings with artsy façades, fine iron details on balconies.. bow-windows.. curvy decorations inspired by nature.. I also love Parisian vintage cafés and restaurants full of beautiful details from the bar to the restroom!

Then.. I found out about Art Nouveau and Art Deco styles!

Czech this out!

To be honest, the first time I realised what is Art Nouveau was during my first trip to Prague, the stunning Czech capital full of beauty in every corner.. I discovered Mucha’s unique collection in Mucha’s museum. He is the master of Art Nouveau with his famous vintage advertisement posters. (The Dancing lady is my favorite! 😍)

A few years later, I visited NYC and was hit by its iconic Art Deco skyscrapers, the ones that we all surely saw somewhere on the Hollywood movies and TV series..

Some Art history

Art Nouveau was created by a bunch of artists like Guimard and Horta in late 19th century, and spread in Paris and all over Europe since Universal Exhibition of 1900. It was the new alternative style to classic Haussmannian architecture with much less constraints, more freedom and creativity.

Art Nouveau is all about pastel colors, floral decorations, curves and mosaics.

After the World War I, in the thirties, it was progressively replaced by its successor: Art Deco.

Art Deco is all about precise geometric shapes and strong colors.

In this article, I made a list of the most beautiful Art Nouveau & Art Deco spots I came across in Europe and on the other side of the Atlantic. Enjoy 😉

Paris x Art Nouveau

First things first, the iconic Subway entrance “bouche de métro Guimard“

La Samaritaine, the majestic fancy shopping center, restored after 16 years of renovation (located in 1st district between Rue de Rivoli and La Seine)

Felix Potin building, it used to be in my neighbourhood for many years and I enjoyed it every time I go by (Rue de Rennes, 6th district).

Other beautiful buildings:

  • Immeuble Lavirotte with its famous brown door (avenue Rapp, 7th district).
  • Castel Béranger (16th district)

Enough of roaming! Let’s enjoy the evening somewhere nice! (PS: get dressed up)

NYC x Art Deco

The Chrysler building is my favourite one!

Other remarkable buildings:

  • One World Trade Center
  • Empire State Building
  • NYSE building

Bonus for Art Nouveau Lovers: Budapest

Budapest is not only the capital of Hungary, it’s also known as the capital of Art Nouveau!

Indeed, while strolling the city, I was really enchanted by the beauty of the buildings façades. It’s like a time-travel to the roaring twenties!

If you visit, don’t miss the New York Café and the Gellert thermal baths, two masterpieces of Art Nouveau! 😉
