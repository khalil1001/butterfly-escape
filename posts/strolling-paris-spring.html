Strolling Paris Cobblestones by Spring Times

May 1, 2025

← Back to all articles

Enjoying the little things that Spring season brings to Paris is my favorite hobby during warm days, when nature starts having bright colors and the weather being more clement. It’s like Paris gets dressed with its most beautiful dress. That almost gives me butterflies! And I say to myself “Lady just get your trench, your fine rouge à lèvre and be infinitely Parisienne.”

I mean I love getting lost in cobblestones streets, spending afternoons in parcs, sunset gazing, pique-niques over la Seine, grabbing coffee in terrasses and people watching…

Here I will share one of my favorite balades over Quartier Latin & Le Marais. Enjoy 🦋

Start at Quai Henri IV by having a nice walk over la Seine until you reach Bassin de l’Arsenal (15 mins walk). Enjoy the quay and its floating boats and continue strolling its cobblestones until the well-known Place de la Bastille (15 mins walk).

Go through Rue des Tournelles to reach Place des Vosges (<10 mins walk).

It’s one of my favorite gems over the area. I love laying in the park in front of its Fontaine, I always get myself a coffee to go and/or a pastry from one of the Boulangeries of the hood and just chill. For a more fancy break, you can sit in one of the terrasses nearby. After caffeine/sugar fuelling, go for a session of Art Window browsing through the several galleries that surround La Place des Vosges.

Go through Rue Beautrellis then Rue Charles V until you reach Le Village Saint-Paul (5 mins walk), a very artsy and unique spot with tiny shops, galleries, bistrots and restaurants. A little village among the big City.

If you like garden and Castel vibes, check on Hôtel de Sully and continue strolling in the Marais.

The bridges around are the perfect spot for a romantic and breathtaking views… Especially Pont de Sully… So Strike a pose!

To continue on the village vibe, reach out to La Rue des deux Ponts, through Quai de Bethune (10 mins walk). Literally “a bridge between worlds”. Nestled on the charming Ile Saint-Louis, it connects two bridges Pont Marie & Pont de la Tournelle. I love this street for its medieval artistic vibes, its old buildings with iron balconies and warm-toned façades. Not a very long promenade but full of character and charm.

For the most motivated, I can add a completely different spot to eat local and world street food, Le marché des enfants rouges (20 mins walk from Pont Marie). The perfect spot for food lovers where you can taste different types of dishes, get some fresh fruit if you want. With that, you would complete your 30K steps a day for sure! 😉

Finally, get a break under some tree shadow in the little park « Square du Temple – Elie Wiesel ».

To sum-up my day-balade Inspo on a warm spring day step by step :

  • Quai Henri IV
  • Pont de l’Arsenal
  • Place de la Bastille
  • Rue des Tournelles
  • Place des Vosges
  • Rue Beautrellis
  • Rue Charles V
  • Village Saint Paul
  • Cours et Jardin de l’Hôtel de Sully
  • Pont de Sully
  • Quai de Béthune
  • Pont de La Tournelle
  • Rue des deux ponts
  • Pont Marie
  • Marché des enfants rouges
  • Square du Temple – Elie Wieselw
