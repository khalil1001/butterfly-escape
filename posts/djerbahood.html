🇹🇳 Djerba

Let's get lost in DJERBAHOOD!

January 8, 2021

← Back to all articles

Djerba, “the island of dreams”, is a tunisian little heaven between Earth and sea. When I see its water turquoise-blue and its golden sand made up of little shells, Djerba is like a gorgeous mediterranean mermaid for me.

I love the Djerbian landscapes: sunset lovers would probably watch one of the most amazing sunsets ever!

As located in the south of Tunisia near the desert, it’s where you can slow down and breathe fresh air in the oasis.

No wonders, it’s an amazing destination for relaxing holidays and a good dose of vitamin D. But not only… 😉

I had the chance to visit in early 2020 before pandemic, and I was so glad to reconnect with the Djerbian mood especially with the traditional architecture: the beautiful traditional white houses (called “Houch” in tunisian dialect), the tiny streets and cafés in old town where you can sit and enjoy the simple little things. And above all, I discovered an amazing neighbourhood full of beauty, art, good taste and energy!

Just Imagine…

– 1 paradisiac island 🏝

– 1 little old village 🏘

– 150 artists 🎨

– 30 nationalities 🗺

– shining sun, colourful facades and bougainvillea all around! 🌸

Djerbahood is a worldwide panorama of Street Art, a major place of expression for artists from diverse cultures.

It’s set-up in “Erriath”, the old town in the heart of the island. I enjoyed walking through the narrow streets, watching the colourfoul traditional tunisian doors, admiring those marvelous pieces of art, and wondering around this unique open-sky museum…

This is my collection among the 250 artworks of the hood. So, enjoy the tour 😉

While strolling through those alleys I admired the arabic calligraphy art, work of the brilliant artist El Seed and other calligraphists…

And while checking on a random half-open painting workshop, I had the chance to meet the talented artist Tahar Aouida. I enjoyed discovering some of his stunning paintings and even got a little personalized calligraphy painting as a souvenir! This was such a nice totally unexpected meeting, and the best way to finish the tour on a high note! 🙂

For now, it’s time for a little break. So let’s go grab a good cup of turkish coffee somewhere cosy 😉
