Strolling San Francisco Hills & Hoods

September 22, 2024

← Back to all articles

« Don’t go on a round-the-world trip, take a plane ticket to SF. »

I had the chance to explore SF earlier this autumn, aka the best season to visit the city. I strolled its 50 hills up and down and I enjoyed its amazing cultural diversity through food and authentic architecture in every corner.

In this article, I will take you through my photos to the hoods I loved the most. Enjoy 🫶🏻

SF Downtown 🌆

A vibrant area that offers a mix of different experiences :

  • Exploring the Union Square (fancy shopping and Art galleries) & Financial District (iconic skyscrapers like Transamerica Pyramid & Salesforce Tower)
  • Trying local and special flavors in the Ferry Building Foodcourt, a foodie heaven with nice view on the Bay bridge (time to enjoy Empanadas Argentinas from📍El Porteño /& chessecake from 📍Cheesequakes 👌🏻)
  • Strolling the Embarcadero and its scenic waterfront area
  • -for Art lovers- enjoying SFMOMA (museum with an impressive collection of contemporary artworks)

North beach 🇮🇹 

Ciao! Bienvenuti! If you have not yet understood that you are in SF « Little Italy » , the Italian flags and colors all around will remind you!

Strolling the Columbus Ave and Vallejo St made me feel the dolce vita energy especially on a sunny day: I started by grabbing an Espresso at Caffe Trieste, the most iconic and famous vintage music bar. You can meet local artists and enjoy the old celebrities photos there. Then, I got myself a Cannolo from the Italian pastry shop next door.

The nighttime is noisy and fun with lots of street artists, theater shows and full ristorantes! 🤌🏻🤌🏻

The Sentinel Building is quite impressive especially by night.

Chinatown 🇨🇳 

Located in the heart of the Downtown, it’s the oldest and largest Chinatown outside of China.

Get lost in the Grant Ave, the street full of shops, restaurants and historic buildings, and don’t miss the Dragon Gate entrance.

Another nice way to explore the hood : California Street Cable Car.

Japan town 🇯🇵 

For the most authentic Japanese vibes : yummy street food, tasty matcha and amazing details through every facade.

Don’t skip :

  • « Peace Pagoda » temple
  • « Nijiya » Super Market
  • « Forest Books » bookstore
  • « Japan Center Malls » (shopping & dining)
  • 📍Maruwu Seicha (tasty matcha & pastries)
  • 📍Yama-chan (authentic street food « Takoyaki »)
  • 📍Mochill (mochidonut = mochi x donut)

Mission Dolores 🇲🇽 

Quite colorful neighborhood with Latin American vibes, nice Taquerías, street art all around, vintage & artistic shops, concept stores…

Don’t miss :

  • 📍Dolores Park, for chilling with family or friends especially at Sunset
  • 📍Dolores Park cafe, for brunching or remote working

Haight-Ashbury ☮️

Vibrant and colorful neighborhood. The symbol of the 1960s contreculture movement and the Summer of Love.

With plenty of Street Art, Vintage and photography shops, it’s worth streets-strolling, people-watching and simply soaking in the free spirit of the neighborhood.

  • 📍Buena Vista Park
  • 📍710 Collective

Marina 🌴

A charming neighborhood with lively atmosphere and Californian vibes.

Don’t skip :

  • strolling the 📍Chestnut St and its facades (trendy boutiques, coffee shops and restaurants)
  • walking and biking at the 📍Crissy Field (nice views of the Golden Gate Bridge)
  • relaxing at the 📍Palace of Fine Arts and its lagoon
  • Mission revival buildings (Presidio Theatre)
