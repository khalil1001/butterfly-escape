Corfu, the Emerald Island

September 18, 2022

← Back to all articles

Kaliméra.. Yassou .. Efkaristó.. Say you are in Greece, without saying you’re in Greece… and No, it’s not about Santorini or Mykonos. Even though it’s not world-widely known, Corfu is a very enjoyable greek island. It’s quite calm and romantic, yet noisy and full of life. The diversity of its culture and origins is very remarkable and the contrast of its nature is just mind-blowing to me. It’s a perfect destination for curious travellers, nature lovers and Lovers! ❤️

I would recommend 6 days at least to enjoy a bit of Corfu, in low season. Here’s a little insight of my trip, moments I captured through my lens and spots I enjoyed the most. Enjoy!

Some Geography

Corfu is the capital of Ionian Islands. It’d located on the west side of Greece, in the heart of the Ionian Sea. It’s very close to Albania, and not far from south Italy. Its nature is so spectacular, with its coastline, lakes, lagoons, forests and mountains…

Back to origins

Unlike the major part of Greece, Corfu was not colonized by the Ottoman Empire. It was under the Romans, the Byzantine empire, the Venetian domination, the French occupation, the British rule and the United States of Ionian Islands. This diversity is very notable through the old buildings of the Corfu town Kerkyra. According to Greek Mythology, the Greek name of Corfu « Kerkyra », came from the Nymph Corcyra, the daughter of the river god Asopos. She was kidnapped by the sea god Poseidon who brought her here and gave her name to the island.

Strolling in Kerkyra

Kerkyra is the central town of the island. It’s full of life by day and night. You can notice its old Venetian Citadelle from afar, overlooking the sea and the port. It’s formed of two main sides: the Old town and the new town.

The Old Town

The old town is a Unesco world heritage. I enjoyed strolling its narrow cobblestone streets, staring at its old colorful buildings, the neighborhood washing lines and the bougainvillea all around..

Although strongly influenced by the Venetians, it’s amazing how diversified the architecture is: a concentration of Baroque, Renaissance and British all in once, along with Orthodox Churches in every street corner.

Whenever I need a little break, I can get myself a Kumqum cake, the corfiot pastry based on Kumquat fruit.

Near Spianada square, there’s the Liston, the popular pedestrian street in French-style. It was inspired from the famous parisian street Rue de Rivoli.

The new town

The modern town is structured in urban large squares with fashion stores and shops.

For culture lovers, there are many monuments worth visiting in Kerkyra. For my part, I would have enjoyed visiting the Byzantine museum.

Farniente, the Greek way

Like most of the Mediterraneans, the Greeks know how to simply enjoy life. And this is a too good place not to: crystal clear waters, sunny days, beautiful sunsets, fresh and colorful food… And what a delight!

Greek Food

I like hanging out in Tavernas. A Taverna is a small traditional greek restaurant that serves traditional dishes and is always a family business. You can often see the owner (father or grandpa) sitting near the checkout, the Chef (wife, daughter or sister), cooking.. both smiling and offering a warm welcome to visitors. The vintage decorations and the homemade dishes made me feel welcomed at their own house. I enjoy trying traditional greek dishes full of flavors like Meze, Souvlaki, Moussaka… on top of that the famous Greek Coffee.

In addition to the Greek coffee, the Ice Coffee is very popular. In summer, it’s very common to see people hanging out or chilling on the beach with their take-away cup of Café Frappé, Cappuccino Freddo or Espresso Freddo. As a coffee lover, I enjoyed this so much! They had it all right! 😄

On the other hand, the greek breakfast is usually salty, based on eggs and cheese pie or spinach pie. If you’d rather have a sweet breaky, look for a fancy coffeehouse. 🙂

Dreamy Corfiot Paralias

The Paralias (beaches) and their sceneries are incredibly beautiful, especially thanks to the lush green vegetation all around.

The water is so pure that you can see colorful fishes with the naked eye. I saw many of these cuties in beaches of Paleokastritsa like Agios Spiridon & Ampelaki beach…

Some of them are only accessible by boat, like Arias beach. The boat cruise is a fun way to discover many hidden gems on one day trip! 😉

On the north side, between Cape Drastis and Peroulades, there is the famous Channel of Love or Canal d’Amour (Kanali tou Erota). This place is so unreal! With its sandstone rocks and crystal clear blue water, it left me speechless!

However, it’s a very famous resort, so would probably be crowded in high-season. But, it’s really worth it.

What else ?

There are many beautiful lakes in the island like Lagoon of Antiniotis or Lake Antinioti near Agios Spiridon Beach. It’s connected directly to the sea through sand dunes. I enjoyed hiking in its forest and taking long walks around it. I always meet fishermen peacefully sitting on its river.

Practical tips for a friend 😉

Don’t get asleep in the plane

If you take a flight from Paris to join Corfu, please take a seat on the right side of the plane! The landscape is absolutely gorgeous and you can see Venise from above.

Road-trip idea (10 days)

Spend 2 days in each of these spots and explore the life around:

  • Kerkyra (culture & downtown noisy life)
  • Gouvia (calm nature, marina & night life)
  • Acharavi (wild life near Albania)
  • Sidari (spectacular coast and chill)
  • Pelokastritsá (dreamy hilly beaches)

Find good food in:

  • Bakalogatos (Kerkyra)
  • Taverna THOMAS (Kommeno)
  • Avàlle Bistro (Gouvia)
  • Taverna HELLAS (Perithia)
  • Pyramid (Agios Spyridonas)
  • Yiannis Taverna (Krouzeri)

Internet is mostly down…

From my personal experience, internet was not working very well. It’s very slow or almost down in most areas. 🤷🏻‍♀️

Transportation

The transportation is a point to figure out seriously as it’s an island and the public transport is not that good.. You have three options : renting a car, or better a scooter or a quad. Scooters and quads are used by lots of locals daily. They can be more practical to join hidden spots on hills and nice beaches that could be hardly reached by car. Anyways, you’d better be an experienced driver as the roads are quite dizzy and high. Unfortunately Greece is the 4th most dangerous country in the world on the road. So choose the option that suits you the most and be safe. 🙂 Otherwise, the public blue and green buses could be an option as they go to most known spots, but like greek people, they are not in hurry! 😄 The buses are always late and could never show up! For my part, I experienced the scooter and the quad and it was quite fun!

Mosquitos & Bees…

There are lots of them everywhere in the island. Hopefully, they don’t like you as much as they like me! 😉

Last but not least, Corfu is very beautiful island and a budget-friendly destination where you can fully enjoy life, chill and practice nice nature outgoing and activities.
