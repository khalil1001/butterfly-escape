🇭🇺 Budapest

"Cool" Escape to Buda-Pest

December 15, 2021

← Back to all articles

I have had Budapest on my “Christmas trips” bucketlist for a long time, as it’s famous for its city lights and Christmas markets during december. I finally had the chance to visit and I was more than enchanted! The festive mood all around is very cheerful, but there’s much more to see and do! I was particularly amazed by the stunning beauty of the city and its incredible contrasts. I felt on connection with Budapest right away. It’s the city of nostalgy and diversity. I didn’t just travel in Budapest, I time-traveled! It has such a vibe that is historical and full of life, cultural and fun all at once!

Cool Cool breeze!

No surprise when visiting in december a central-european city, the weather could be very cold and especially windy. It could even snow, depending on the Christmas magic! 😉 But it’s totally worth it to me. There are lots of indoor activities, and the transportation is so common and fun with easy access to any area! I enjoyed taking fun rides with the traditional yellow tram around the Danube river: Tram number 2 could take you until the beautiful hungarian parliament, and Tram number 47 could cross the stunning bridge of Liberty. It’s actually my favorite bridge.

Whenever I start strolling the Christmas markets or walking around the Danube, I protect myself well from the cold breeze, make sure to have my hot chocolate in hands (or latte or any preferable hot beverage..) and enjoy the walk. 🙂

Back to the origins..

Budapest is the capital of Hungary. It was created in late 19th century with the merger of the 3 cities: Buda, Pest and Óbuda. And it had different historical points: the Roman Empire, the Austro-Hungarian Empire and by 16th century the Ottoman Empire. It was partially germanized, then communist then shaped with original Haussmanian buildings. That’s why it’s also called the “Paris of the East”. In a word, it’s such a melting-pot of cultures and dynasties.

Stunning architecture

Budapest was not built on one day and you can see it when you start strolling its streets. The diversity of architecture styles is very remarkable, from the medieval buildings to the modern ones… and in between, the renaissance, neo-romanesque, neo-gothic, Art-Nouveau, baroque, Haussmanian.. You can also notice the old grey and sad communist buildings.

I admired the neo-gothic building of Hungarian parliament inside-out, it’s actually the third largest parliament building in the world!

The Danube bridges are magnificent, walking through the iconic Chain bridge or the Bridge of Liberty is like walking in a movie scene. You can admire them from top of the hillside or by going on a cruise.

I particularly loved the Art-Nouveau buildings and enjoyed sitting in the cafés and admiring their elegant vintage style, golden decorations and old paintings: it’s like being in an Opera house.. It felt like living in the roaring twenties!

Day in Buda, Night in Pest

There are lots of things to see and do. The best way to discover the city is starting the visit in the morning on the historical side (Buda) then going down the river and enjoying the panorama until reaching the downtown (Pest) by night (sunset is around 4pm 🙂).

For me, Buda, imperial and baroque, is like Jazz. Pest, busy and full of life is like Swing.

Buda is the old town perched on the hillside over the river, on the catsle district side, where you can find the Bourgeoise villas and elegant baroque residences. I liked the funicular ride to arrive at the Fisherman’s bastion, the stunning neo-romanesque/neo-gothic monument with its seven towers. It offers a breathtaking view over Budapest. It’s a real life fairytale. It’s actually rumored that it inspired the Walt Disney logo!

Pest is the downtown full of life, with its Christmas markets, gothic churches, theaters, cabarets, jewish quarter, rooftops, coffeeshops…

Like in a Christmas movie..

The beautiful lights and Christmas decorations are in every corner! The colourful Christmas markets around the center are full of flavours and scents. The people are enjoying the music and the street food. I tried the traditional hungarian pastry “Chimney cake”, it’s very tasty!

I liked strolling the fairy Fashion Street and wandering around the Ferris Wheel. I even enjoyed a part of the movie “Home Alone” that was projected in the middle of the street near the wheel. 🙂

What else?

Experiencing thermal baths of Budapest is quite fun, relaxing and healthy! The city has more than 1000 natural sources with healing properties, and lots of spa complexes with indoor and outdoor pools. I enjoyed a lot the Szécheny and Gellert baths, as well as their Art-Nouveau buildings.
