🇮🇹 Amalfi

Amal-Fi Coast

July 14, 2022

← Back to all articles

Italy is my sweet european escape. I love it for its beautiful landscapes, stunning architecture, tasty food…and above all its lovely culture and lifestyle. La Felicita.. La Bella Vita.. Il Dolce Farniente.. Mangiare, Bere, Fare L’amore.. E BASTA!

I have been to wide-known italian cities like Rome, Venezia and Florence. They are coulourful and full of life. It’s a nice citybreak and a magnificent cultural escape. However, I believe you would not really experience the italian way of life unless you take a trip to the Coastline and be in touch with locals, discover the italian countryside while enjoying a car ride to the beach…

Today, I’m sharing my first trip to the Amalfi Coast. At first sight, it seems like a fancy trip to Capri and Positano, full of tourists and Instagrammable spots.. Actually, it was the most atypical trip I would imagined. The whole story began when I just finished my internship, I decided to take a last minute plane ticket from Paris to Napoli, and convinced my friend to come with me. All we wanted is to explore Italian summer.

So, we landed in Napoli on a hot sunday morning with our backpacks, with no plan! We first took a walk in the city and went along the bay of Naples where the Mount Vesuvius rises above with its active volcano. To be honest, that would be the most interesting thing we saw in Naples as we went directly in some wifi area to search for an affordable shelter for tonight. And.. what a struggle! It’s actually the hot touristic season and finding a hotelroom or an airbnb near the wide-known spots was very tough. We started looking a bit far from those areas.. nothing! Well, we desperately managed to find a homestay in Pimonte, somewhere between Amalfi and Sorrento. The map shows 30 kms, well, we went for it…

So to make it short, it took us 5 hours to reach our stay adress, which is actually at the top of the hills, the road is very dizzy and the public transport is not quite good. We changed the train then found no bus available at the evening so we managed to convince a taxi driver to give us a ride for the double price… and we finally arrived, tired but happy!

Benny, is the italian guy that welcomed us, showed us our studio located in the house where he lives with his wife, little daughter and parents. No one speaks english or french, they only speak italian, a language we are not very familiar with. But, we managed to communicate, mainly that italian people are very expressive, talk with their hands a lot. Benny showed us the nearest and (probably) the only restaurant in the neighborhood. It’s where I ate one of the most delicious pizzas ever! one huge pizza that could be shared by two persons for only 3.5€. We were the only ones in the restaurant. The waiter showed us a spot nearby with a view on the bay of Naples and the Vesuvius. That was nice.

Day 1 Amalfi

We were suspicious about the transportation and asked our host for recommendation. He introduced us to his neighbor Vincenzo who could share a ride with us on his way to Amalfi. He was a student and that was his summer job. Well, we said YES!

It’s 8 a.m in the morning. Vincenzo came with his old white Fiat 500 with his parents. We shared a dizzy road and went down the mountain full of forests to reach the sea. As we approach Amalfi, the landscape changes. The panorama was so spectacular with shades of blue and green. We loved those sceneries as well as the company of our italian neighbors. Vincenzo was fluent in english, gave us some recommendations and told us about tourist traps to avoid. He is a student in Economy and he does many little jobs in summer season to finance his studies. He had a girlfriend and his dream was to take her for a romantic trip to Paris! ❤️

We finally arrived in Amalfi. The city center is very gorgeous with its huge Cathedral « Duomo di Sant Andrea ». We enjoyed walking in the center. Of course, it’s full of tourists and travellers, but really worth it. We took a cappuccino in the piazza in front of the Cathedral, then headed to the beach, chilling all day out there before going back to our stay.

It’s 9 p.m. We are back home at evening, the neighborhood is very quiet. It’s crazy how the atmosphere is different from where we were a couple of hours ago. We said ‘Ciao’ to the « nonno », the grandpa staying on his chair at the entry of the house, the father of our host Benny. We took a shower and went back to the same restaurant of yesterday. Unfortunately, it was already closed, it’s too late for restaurants to be still open by 10 p.m.. We had no idea where to find another one, or if we could be delivered. We took a walk around, only little houses with « verandas » and families reunited, talking and laughing. We could smell jasmine.

We asked our host if we could have dinner somewhere, (ristorante per mangiare?), he smiled and invited us to share dinner with his family. We were a bit unconfortable to crash into their house like that. He was actually the owner of the supermarket in front of the house, but it was already closed. So we asked him if we could buy something from the supermarket to help us out. It was offered!

Now, it’s time to plan our second day. We asked Vincenzo if we can share another car ride together. He was busy…

Day 2 Capri

It’s 8 a.m. Despite our nature, we were early birds. We headed to the bus station. We wanted to visit Positano or Capri. The next bus arrives in 1 hour.. not even sure about where it goes, but it should go down in the direction of the coast.. In the meantime, we searched for a coffeeshop. We went to the first bakery and ordered 2 Lattes and some pastries. The bakery lady tried to communicate with us with a mix of Italian and english, to ask us who we were visiting in Pimonte. We said, no one we came for travel (tourists). She smiled « Aaaaaaahhhh tourists…hummm »…

While we were staying in the terrasse of the bakery, someone riding a car made a stop just close to us and shout our names with an Itaaaaalian accent. It was Benny, our host. From his face expression, we understood that he was very surprised and he asked us where are we going, a discussion where the bakery lady took part as a translator. 😄 « Positano or Capri », we said. « Aaaaahhhh Maaaamma Mia… » He was not very enthousiastic, and that made us a bit unconfortable « …???… ».

He suggested to give us a ride to the ferry station, where we could take a boat to Capri. Positano seemed not like a good idea… Well, we said YES!

After our boat ride, we finally arrived to Capri, the island of love. What a dreamy place! 😍

Day 3 Ravello

On our first day in Amalfi, we noticed buses riding to « Ravello » full of people. We checked it out and it seemed a beautiful must-see spot. So, we decided to go back. We took that bus. Waw! The road is really dizzy, I would not recommend it for vertiginous people. And the place was really worth it. A gorgeous village at the top of mountain, and the top of the clouds. Very beautiful and very peaceful.

Day 4 Sorrento

It’s our last day here. We knew how long is the way back to Naples airport. So as usual, we were early birds. We said goodbye to our lovely hosts. Took the train until Sorrento, a beautiful place with a cute marina. Took a break there, then headed to Naples.

I loved this trip. It was quite an adventure, and will definitely come back to slowly enjoy the life here, take more boat trips, go for some hiking, and explore more beautiful places like Procida and Positano.
