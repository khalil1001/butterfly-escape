Into Hygge, the Danish Pursuit of Happiness

May 6, 2025

← Back to all articles

Copenhagen is now the world’s most liveable city according to the Economist Intelligence Unit’s 2025 global liveability index.

It’s not the first time I hear about how happy the Danes are… well, it always intrigued me how come people living in nordic city where the weather is nasty most time of the year and sunlight is low, are often ranked among the happiest people in the world ?

I heard it’s all about Hygge.. hmm, another strange concept of some quirky people trying to sell some design stuff and fancy candles ?

When I traveled to Copenhague, I visited the Happiness Museum and discovered another concept “Lykke” : Happiness.

So how is “Hygge” making the “Lykke” of the Danes?

According to Meik Wiking, the CEO of the Happiness research Institute of CPH, “Hooga or Heurgh.. It’s not important how you choose to pronounce it. What is important is how you feel it. Whether you’re cuddled up on a sofa with a loved one, or sharing comfort food with your closest friends, hygge is about creating an atmosphere where you can let your guard down.”

“It’s easy to see why Danemark is often called the world’s happiest country. Not only do they have work and life in perfect balance, free higher education and trains that run on time, but they burn more candles per household than anywhere else.”

Let’s dig deeper.

Hygge is both a verb and an adjective. something can be “hyggelig/hyggelight” (hygge-like): What a hyggelig living room! or How hyggelight it will be to get together on Friday!

Hygge is a key performance indicator of most Danish social gatherings. The Danes are obsessed with cosiness and would recommend a restaurant or a café based mostly on how “hyggelig” it is. It means that everything we learned on marketing class are wrong ?! “Price, product and promotion can kiss my ass. It’s all about Hygge here”

Hygge Manifesto

  • Atmosphere (turn down the lights, burn some candles)
  • Presence (turn off the phones, be here now)
  • Pleasure (coffee, chocolate..)
  • Gratitude
  • Harmony (no competition, no need to brag about achievements)
  • Comfort (get comfy and relax)
  • Truce (No drama, let’s discuss politics another day)
  • Togetherness (relationships and stories)
  • Shelter (the place of peace and security)

Hygge and Lykke (Hooga & Looka)

The classical way to measure the success of a society is the GDP (Gross Domestic Product), based on economic growth. But since many years, researches for happiness have evolved, from the Gross Happiness Index created in Bhutan, to other indices of happiness that explore the reasons of why some people or countries are happier than others: “genetics, relationships, health, income, job, sense of purpose and freedom”.

When it comes to why Danemark does so well in international happiness surveys is the welfare state(which seems not perfect but really good). It clearly reduces stress and uncertainty among the population: Universal and free healthcare, free university education, generous unemployment benefits… besides, the trust is at its higher level in Danemark, you can see lots of pushchairs parked outside cafés when the parents are inside drinking coffee.. there’s also a high level of freedom reported by the Danes in terms of wealth and a good civil society.. All of these factors are common with other nordic countries that are often well ranked too (Sweden, Finland, Norway and Iceland). However, maybe the “hygge” is what sets Danemark apart from the rest of the Nordic countries.

Finally, “Hygge and Happines might be linked as hygge may be the persuit of everyday happiness.”

Last but not least, this article was inspired by my trip to Copenhagen and based on The Little book of hygge by Meik Wiking.
